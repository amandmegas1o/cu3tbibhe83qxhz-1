<p><p>The craft beer market is primarily divided into two types: ales and lagers. Ales are fermented at warmer temperatures, resulting in a rich, fruity flavor profile, with popular styles including IPAs and stouts. Lagers, on the other hand, are fermented at cooler temperatures, leading to a clean, crisp taste, characterized by styles such as pilsners and bocks. This distinction allows breweries to cater to diverse consumer preferences while driving innovation and creativity within the craft beer industry.</p></p>
